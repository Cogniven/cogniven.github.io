Arkham Narrative Conversion

A project for turning play sessions of Arkham Horror into narrative material through recording, transcription, restructuring, and later AI-assisted rewriting.

Pre-start Updated: 2026-04-12 Single Project
Premise Workflow Current Stage Extensions

Section 01

Premise

This project begins from actual play. The goal is not only to record what happened in the game, but to turn the session into narrative material that can later be rewritten and expanded.

Play Session as Source Material

active

2026-04-12

The primary material will come from actual play sessions of Arkham Horror, treated as the first layer of narrative input.

Scenario and Rule Context

draft

2026-04-12

Scenario text, card effects, and in-session decisions will be used as supporting context when reconstructing the story.

Section 02

Workflow

The workflow is planned as a staged conversion process: gameplay, recording, transcription, narrative structuring, and later literary rewriting.

Audio Recording Plan

draft

planned

Future sessions will be recorded in audio form so that spoken reactions, decisions, and emergent dramatic moments can be preserved.

Transcription and Story Sorting

draft

planned

Recordings will be converted into text, then reorganized together with scenario material into a readable story sequence.

AI-Assisted Novelization

test

later stage

After the narrative material is stabilized, AI will be used to help rewrite it into a more novel-like form while keeping the original session logic intact.

Section 03

Current Stage

The project is still at the earliest phase. The game has been played, but recording and text conversion have not started yet.

Only Gameplay Completed

active

current

At this moment, the project exists only at the level of actual play experience. No audio archive or text reconstruction has been produced yet.

Method Still Being Fixed

draft

current

The exact method for recording, segmenting, and restructuring the play session is still being defined.
