AI Voice Simulation with DDSP

A continuing experiment in voice dataset preparation, DDSP-based training, inference testing, and iterative audio reconstruction.

Project Note

This project records several attempts to build and refine a voice simulation workflow using DDSP. The focus is not only the final model, but also the dataset decisions, preprocessing choices, failed experiments, and checkpoints that shaped the process.

Latest

Update · 2026-06-13

A new batch of recordings was reviewed today. Most of the material was discarded because the usable portion was too limited or not stable enough for training. One recording was kept for further processing.

The selected recording was split in Adobe Audition, then processed and sliced through MSST before being used for DDSP 6.3 training. After an initial training run, both the source sound and the inference result from the 6000-step checkpoint sounded noticeably muffled.

The current decision is to return to the dataset from the second attempt and retrain it under DDSP 6.3, then compare whether the older dataset produces a clearer inference result than the newly processed recording.

Workflow

Current processing route

Recording Review

Newly collected audio was screened first. Low-value or unstable material was removed before entering the processing chain.

AU Splitting

The remaining recording was split manually in Adobe Audition to prepare cleaner segments for later processing.

MSST Processing

The selected audio was sent into MSST for processing and slicing before entering the training dataset.

DDSP 6.3 Training

The processed slices were used for training in DDSP 6.3, with early inference tested at the 6000-step checkpoint.

History

Development log

Attempt 01 Early DDSP

Initial voice-source experiment

The first attempt used an earlier DDSP workflow and a dubbing voice source. The training ran for roughly 40,000 to 50,000 steps, mainly to test whether the source material could support a usable model.

Attempt 02 DDSP-Barbara 6.1

Second dataset and longer training

The second attempt used DDSP-Barbara 6.1 and reached around 110,000 steps. This dataset is now being reconsidered as a more stable base for retraining under DDSP 6.3.

2026-06-13 DDSP 6.3

New recording test and retraining decision

A new recording was processed through AU and MSST, then used for DDSP 6.3 training. The sound remained muffled, including the 6000-step inference result, so the next step is to retrain the second-attempt dataset with the newer DDSP 6.3 workflow.

Diagnosis

Current judgment

The new recording is not ideal. The retained material was technically usable, but the resulting sound quality was not clear enough. It produced a muffled impression before and after inference.
Dataset quality remains central. The result suggests that model version alone is not enough. Source clarity, segmentation quality, and dataset consistency are still decisive.
The second dataset will be reused. The next experiment will reuse the dataset from the second attempt and retrain it with DDSP 6.3 for comparison.

Next

Return to the stronger dataset.

The next stage is to retrain the second-attempt dataset under DDSP 6.3 and compare its clarity against the discarded new recording path.
