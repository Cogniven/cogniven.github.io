Investment Research System

A structured framework for market observation, allocation logic, and long-term decision making.

Active Updated: 2026-03-22

What This System Is

This page organizes the way I observe markets, structure investment thinking, and track current direction over time.

Core Structure

This system is built around four connected parts: investment framework, market observation, risk structure, and current direction. Together they shape how ideas are filtered, compared, and acted on.

Section 01 — Investment Framework

This section defines the broad logic of allocation, time horizon, and strategic preference. It works as the stable layer of the system.

Section 02 — Market Observation

This section tracks indicators, regime changes, and notable signals. It is used to update judgment without reacting to every short-term fluctuation.

Section 03 — Risk Structure

This section records how exposure is controlled, how positions are sized, and what conditions require reduction or review.

Section 04 — Current Direction

This section summarizes the present stance of the system: what is being watched closely, where conviction is rising, and where uncertainty remains.

Current State

The framework is stable, but the observation layer continues to evolve. Recent work is focused on improving how signals are interpreted across different conditions.

Sources / Inputs

Inputs include market data, macro signals, strategy results, and written notes. These sources are used together rather than in isolation.

Next Step

Refine the observation layer.

The next stage is to improve how signals are ranked and how conviction is updated.
