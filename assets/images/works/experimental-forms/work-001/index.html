Generative Poster Study

A process log exploring layered composition, distortion, and typographic rhythm through iterative poster experiments.

Date 2026
Status Ongoing
Field Visual Experiment
Mode Study

Experiment Note

This experiment began as an attempt to test whether layered type, unstable alignment, and partial distortion could produce a coherent visual rhythm rather than noise.

Intent

Starting Point

The initial question was simple: how far can a poster move away from stable grid logic before it loses internal coherence?

The study focused less on producing a final poster and more on observing how variation, density, and disruption affect legibility and tension.

Iterations

Iteration Log

Version 01

Initial Structure Test

The first version stayed relatively close to a readable composition, using a central block and limited distortion as a baseline.

Version 02

Density and Misalignment

The second version pushed overlapping text and broken alignment further, increasing friction while testing whether the layout could still hold together.

Version 03

Controlled Disruption

The third version reduced some visual noise and shifted toward more deliberate contrast, creating a clearer tension between control and breakage.

Observations

What Emerged

The most useful finding was that disruption becomes more effective when it is locally concentrated rather than globally applied. Total instability reduced tension instead of increasing it.

Another observation was that typographic rhythm depended less on symmetry than on interval control. Even broken layouts needed recurring internal spacing rules.

Next

Next Step

The next phase is to test the same visual logic under stricter color constraints and with more asymmetrical text distribution.

A later branch may also compare manually composed layouts with AI-assisted generative variants to see how each handles rhythm and distortion.
