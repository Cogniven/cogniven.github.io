Monolithic Landing Draft

2026 Layout Direction Homepage

A one-page homepage concept that merged all site sections before the structure was split into focused entries.

Context

This direction tried to maximize overview by placing works, writing, projects, and archive previews on one continuous page. It improved immediate exposure but reduced clarity in depth navigation.

1. Why It Was Attempted

At that stage, the main concern was discoverability. A monolithic page made every category visible without extra clicks and looked efficient for first-time visitors.

2. Why It Was Abandoned

As content volume increased, the page became dense and repetitive. Different content types required different reading speeds and could not share one scroll rhythm effectively.

3. Lasting Insight

The draft still provided a useful reference for section ordering and narrative flow. Its strongest part was top-level sequencing, not single-page containment.
