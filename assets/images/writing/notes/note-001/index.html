On Incomplete Systems

Mar 22, 2026

A short note on why unfinished structures may be more revealing than finished ones.

Some systems only become visible when they fail to close. Completion often smooths over the seams that made the structure intelligible in the first place.

A finished form can appear coherent precisely because its tensions have been concealed. An unfinished one, by contrast, still shows where pressure collects, where decisions remain unresolved, and where its internal logic resists itself.

That is why incompletion is not always a weakness. Sometimes it is the only moment at which a structure can still be read clearly, before polish turns it into surface.
