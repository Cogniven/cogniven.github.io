About

This site is a long-term personal structure for gathering work, ideas, systems, and ongoing lines of inquiry. It is not designed as a finished showcase, but as a space where research, technical construction, and creative practice can remain in relation to one another. Some works appear as resolved pieces; others remain partial, provisional, or in development. What matters here is not only the completed result, but also the logic, form, and continuity behind it.

Profile

My work moves across several adjacent domains: research, programming, design, writing, and experimental making. These areas are not treated as separate identities, but as connected ways of thinking and building. At times the work takes the form of a visual piece, a written argument, a technical system, or an auditory experiment; at other times it exists as a method, a framework, or an unfinished study. Across these forms, the underlying interest remains consistent: how structure can hold complexity without losing clarity.

Focus

The work collected here generally develops along a few recurring directions. One is analytical: reading, comparing, synthesizing, and shaping ideas into intelligible form. Another is technical: building tools, systems, and coded environments that can be tested, revised, and extended over time. A third is creative: working through image, sound, text, and form to explore expression under structure rather than outside it. These directions often overlap, and the most meaningful projects usually emerge where they meet.

Approach

I tend to work by constructing frameworks first. Categories, systems, and interfaces are not added after the fact; they are part of how the work becomes thinkable. This means revision is central. Pieces may be rewritten, regrouped, reclassified, expanded, or removed entirely. The aim is not constant accumulation, but a more coherent arrangement of what deserves to remain. In that sense, this site functions less like an archive of everything and more like an edited field of attention.
