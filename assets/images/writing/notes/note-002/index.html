On Diluted Expression

Apr 2026 · Note

On how ideas can be expressed without being fully exposed.

Years ago, I had the idea of refining my own writing into a more condensed form. Not to simplify it, but to strip it down to structure — to keep the meaning while reducing the surface.

At the time, this was difficult to execute. Writing tends to expand itself. Once a sentence is started, it often carries more detail than intended, and the act of editing becomes another layer of rewriting.

Now, with AI, this process becomes possible. I can keep only a rough outline — a compressed version of what I want to express — and let another system complete the wording, the rhythm, and the surface form.

This creates a separation between structure and expression. What is written is no longer a direct exposure of the original thought, but a mediated version of it. In that sense, the text becomes less personal, or at least less directly traceable.

However, this distance is only partial. As the amount of writing increases, patterns accumulate. Even if each piece is individually reduced, the collection as a whole begins to reconstruct the underlying structure.

Over time, repetition forms consistency, and consistency forms a recognizable system. What was once diffused across many fragments gradually becomes concentrated again.

So this approach does not remove exposure. It only delays it. The structure remains there, and given enough material, it will surface again.
