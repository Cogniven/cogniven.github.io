Trading Strategy Evaluation

2026 Essay Strategy Evaluation

A note on common performance metrics, principal-anchored drawdown, and recovery exposure in strategy evaluation.

Abstract

This piece starts from common indicators such as Sharpe ratio, Calmar ratio, and maximum drawdown, then points out a problem in finite backtests: the equity curve may conceal the difference between compound growth and principal risk. It proposes a supplementary perspective based on principal anchoring, recovery time, and recovery opportunity exposure.

Open PDF

1. Motivation

Conventional evaluation metrics summarize the observed equity curve, but they do not always show whether a strategy is robust under principal-anchored risk. A strategy that looks acceptable after compounding may still expose the initial capital to a much larger effective loss.

2. Core Question

The central question is not only how much the strategy earns, but how the strategy repairs damage after loss. Recovery time, average holding period, and entry frequency together determine how many realistic recovery opportunities the strategy has after drawdown.
