Nobel Reading Project

A long-term reading plan for past Nobel Prize laureates, awarded works, lectures, and the ideas behind them.

Field Reading / Commentary
Status In Progress
Scope Nobel Prizes
Output Short Reviews

Project Note

This project is a reading plan for Nobel-related works and materials. It does not aim to produce exhaustive academic studies. Instead, it records short, readable reviews that make important works easier to approach, compare, and remember.

Focus

Readable reviews of awarded works

The project follows different Nobel Prize categories and reads selected works, speeches, lectures, papers, or representative materials from past laureates.

The actual reviews will be placed in the Writing section. This page only defines the plan, method, and structure of the project.

Structure

Prize Categories

Literature

Reviews of awarded literary works, Nobel lectures, major writings, and the literary ideas behind each prize.

Peace

Reviews of speeches, public actions, historical contexts, and the debates surrounding peace-related awards.

Sciences

Physics, Chemistry, and Physiology or Medicine, approached through clear explanations of discoveries and their meaning.

Economic Sciences

Reviews of major theories, books, papers, and intellectual traditions connected to laureates in Economic Sciences.

Materials

Materials and Reviews

Each review may begin from a Nobel lecture, an awarded work, a representative book, a major article, an official prize summary, or later discussion around the laureate.

Reviews will be organized by prize category and then by year, so the project can grow gradually without losing its structure.

Direction

Current Direction

The first step is to build the project framework and prepare a separate review archive under Writing. Each prize category will later have its own page.

Over time, the project may develop into a larger reading archive, with yearly reviews, comparative notes, and broader maps of awarded ideas.
